Equipment and Setup

The key to becoming a successful bean-bag thrower is to practice, practice, and practice some more. However, without the right equipment and setup, your practice sessions can be unproductive and frustrating. Choosing the right equipment and setting up your practice space correctly can make all the difference in your bean-bag throwing game. When it comes to equipment, it's important to select bean bags that are the right weight and size for your playing style. Regulation size bean bags are typically 6 inches by 6 inches and weigh between 14 and 16 ounces. However, there are also smaller and larger bean bags available that may work better for you. It's a good idea to try a few different sizes and weights to see what feels most comfortable. In addition to bean bags, you'll also need a bean-bag board. Bean-bag boards are typically made of wood or plastic and measure 2 feet by 4 feet. The board should be elevated off the ground and positioned a certain distance from the throwing line – 27 feet for men and 24 feet for women. If you plan on playing indoors, it's a good idea to invest in a set of smaller bean-bag boards. Setting up your practice space is just as important as selecting the right equipment. Make sure your throwing area provides enough space for you to move around freely and avoid any obstacles that may interfere with your game. You should also ensure that the surface you're playing on is level and free from any debris that may affect the movement of the bean bags. By choosing the right equipment and setting up your practice area correctly, you'll be well on your way to improving your bean-bag throwing game. With enough practice, you may even become a bean-bag throwing champion!

Bean-bag Throwing Drills

Bean-bag Throwing Drills that Will Improve Your Competitive Play! If you're looking to up your game and improve your bean-bag throwing skills, exercises and drills can be a great way to train your body and hone your technique. With practice and dedication, these drills can enhance both your hand-eye coordination and throwing accuracy, helping you become a more formidable adversary on the playing field. One of the essential drills for any bean-bag player is practicing your toss. Focus on keeping your elbow close to your body and your wrist straight as you throw. The goal is to maintain a consistent target and develop muscle memory that enables you to achieve an accurate and repeatable throw every time. Aim at different targets to enhance your accuracy by creating a variety of obstacles that mimic game situations. You can set up targets of different shapes using masking tape or chalk on the ground. Vary the distances to mimic competition settings and make sure to throw from different angles to work on adjusting for the wind and other variables. Another way to increase your overall competitiveness is to the perfect the 'air mail' throw, which is a high-arc toss that sends the bag arcing through the air and into the hole. This is a complicated move, but with consistent practice and a sound technique, you can make it a part of your arsenal. In conclusion, spending time on bean-bag throwing drills can make a significant difference in your performance on the game day. Incorporating these drills from time to time in your daily routine can help you stay agile, sharp and hone your physical and mental listening skills. So, order some bean-bags, find some space and a friend, and start practicing to become a bean-bag throwing champ!
