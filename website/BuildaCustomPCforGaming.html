Choose Your Storage

Consider Your Motherboard and Power Supply Your motherboard plays a crucial role in your computer’s overall performance and capabilities. It connects all your components and peripherals, and determines the number and types of ports, expansion slots, and memory your system can accommodate. When selecting your motherboard, look for one that supports the same socket type as your processor and has the features you need, such as integrated Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, or Thunderbolt ports. Similarly, your power supply is another critical component that ensures your system gets the power it needs to run stably and efficiently. It is important to choose a reliable, efficient, and appropriately powered supply that can handle your computer’s demands, including any overclocking you might do. Consider purchasing one with modular cabling and a high rating from a reputable manufacturer.

Assemble Your PC

Select Your Power Supply The power supply is an essential component of any PC build. It provides power to all other components and ensures that they have a stable and reliable source of energy. When selecting a power supply for your build, you'll want to consider a few key factors: 1. Wattage: The wattage of the power supply will determine how much power it can provide to your components. You'll want to make sure that you have enough wattage to support your entire build, but not so much that you're overspending on an unnecessary amount of power. A good rule of thumb is to choose a power supply that has a wattage between 500-750W. 2. Efficiency: Power supplies come with different efficiency ratings, which indicate how efficiently they convert AC power to DC power. Look for a power supply that has an 80 Plus certification, which guarantees at least 80% efficiency. 3. Modular vs. Non-Modular: Modular power supplies have detachable cables, which can be helpful in reducing clutter and improving airflow within your build. However, they do tend to be more expensive than non-modular power supplies. 4. Brand: It's important to choose a reputable brand when selecting a power supply. Some trusted brands include EVGA, Corsair, and Seasonic. Overall, choosing the right power supply is crucial for ensuring the stability and longevity of your PC build. Don't skimp on this component, as a faulty power supply can cause damage to your other components or even your entire system.
