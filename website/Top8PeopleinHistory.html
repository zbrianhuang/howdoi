1. Albert Einstein

Albert Einstein was a German-born theoretical physicist who developed the theory of general relativity, one of the two pillars of modern physics. He is widely considered one of the most influential scientists of the 20th century, and his work has had a profound impact on our understanding of the universe. In addition to his scientific achievements, Einstein was also an outspoken pacifist and civil rights advocate.

2. William Shakespeare

William Shakespeare was an English playwright, poet, and actor widely regarded as the greatest writer in the English language and one of the world's greatest dramatists. His plays have been translated into every major living language and are performed more often than those of any other playwright.

3. Isaac Newton

Isaac Newton was an English mathematician, physicist, and astronomer who is widely regarded as one of the most influential scientists in history. 4. Mahatma Gandhi

Mahatma Gandhi was an Indian independence activist who is best known for his philosophy of nonviolent resistance. He played a key role in the Indian independence movement and is widely regarded as one of the most important figures in Indian history. Gandhi's teachings continue to inspire civil rights and freedom movements around the world.

5. Leonardo da Vinci

Leonardo da Vinci was an Italian Renaissance artist and inventor who is widely regarded as one of the most creative and versatile minds in history. His famous works include the Mona Lisa and The Last Supper, and his inventions ranged from flying machines to war machines.

6. Alexander the Great

Alexander the Great was a king of Macedon who conquered an empire that stretched from Greece to northwest India. He is widely regarded as one of the greatest military commanders in history and is credited with spreading Greek culture throughout the known world.

7. Marie Curie

Marie Curie was a Polish-born physicist and chemist who is widely regarded as one of the most important scientists of the 20th century. She was the first woman to be awarded a Nobel Prize, and her work on radioactivity paved the way for modern nuclear physics and medicine.

8. Nelson Mandela

Nelson Mandela was a South African anti-apartheid revolutionary and politician who served as the President of South Africa from 1994 to 1999. He is widely regarded as one of the most important figures in South African history and is best known for his role in the anti-apartheid movement and his lifelong struggle for social justice and human rights.
