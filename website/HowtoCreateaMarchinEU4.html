What is a March in EU4?

In the game Europa Universalis 4 (EU4), a March is a valuable tool that can help you gain a tactical advantage over your rivals. A March is a vassal state that has been granted special privileges by its overlord, such as increased military autonomy and the ability to raise a larger standing army. A well-managed March can be a powerful ally in times of war, providing a valuable buffer against invasion and a secondary front that can distract your opponents. But creating a March is not a decision to be made lightly. It requires careful consideration of your diplomatic relationship with potential candidates, as well as an understanding of the pros and cons of creating a March. For example, while a March can provide valuable military support, it can also be a drain on your resources and a potential liability if it becomes too powerful. Fortunately, creating a March in EU4 is relatively straightforward. You simply need to have a vassal state that meets the necessary requirements, such as having a military strength of at least 20% of your own. Once you have selected a suitable candidate, you can grant them March status through the Diplomatic Actions menu. Of course, creating a successful March requires more than just following a few simple steps. You must be able to manage your March effectively and maintain a strong diplomatic relationship with them. This can be achieved through careful attention to their needs, such as providing military support, granting subsidies, and keeping them at arm's length from your other vassals. To sum up, a March in EU4 is a powerful tool that can be used to gain a tactical advantage in the game. But, like any tool, it must be used wisely and with care. By following the advice provided here, you can create a successful March and reap the benefits it provides.
